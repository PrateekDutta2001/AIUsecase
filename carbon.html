Foundation

Radiocarbon Dating: The Physics

Radiocarbon dating exploits the known decay rate of Carbon-14 (half-life: 5,730 ± 40 years). When an organism dies, it stops absorbing ¹⁴C; the ¹⁴C/¹²C ratio decreases predictably. Two primary measurement techniques exist:

Conventional Beta Counting
Detects radioactive decay via beta-particle counting. Requires 1–10g material. Measurement: 10–20 days. Precision: ±40–80 years. Lower cost but cannot distinguish ¹⁴C from ¹⁴N isobars.
Accelerator Mass Spectrometry (AMS) ⭐
Directly counts ¹⁴C, ¹²C, ¹³C atoms via tandem electrostatic accelerator. Requires only 1mg. Measurement: 30–60 minutes. Precision: ±20–30 years. Gold standard for archaeological and forensic dating.
The AMS-AI Revolution: AMS machines at Oxford, ETH Zürich, and ANSTO generate hundreds of isotopic ratio measurements daily. AI models trained on these outputs — combined with OxCal calibration curves (IntCal20: 50,000 datapoints, 0–55,000 BP) — are transforming what can be dated, how quickly, and with what uncertainty bounds.

XAI Methods for Carbon Science & Policy

SHAP on DGL-GNN
Attributes national emission forecasts to specific industrial sectors and trade-flow edges. Reveals carbon leakage pathways invisible to aggregate models — critical for designing effective cross-border policy.
Attention Weight Maps
Visualises which GNN message-passing edges and Temporal Transformer time steps most influence a 2030 emission projection. Built into the DGL framework's interpretability module.
Integrated Gradients — U-Net
Pixel-level attribution on TROPOMI satellite tiles showing exactly which spatial regions triggered methane plume detection — enables ground-truthing and legal verification of emission events.
Counterfactual Policy XAI
Generates ministerial briefs: "A 10% renewable energy subsidy in Sector X reduces national CO₂ by 2.3 Gt over 10 years, with SHAP-attributed confidence interval ±0.4 Gt." Links RL actions to explainable outcomes.
LIME — Enoch Dating
Local explanation for individual manuscript dating decisions — which palaeographic style features and ¹⁴C probability mass most shifted the Bayesian fusion output for a specific scroll.
Causal DAGs
Structural causal models layered on DGL outputs distinguish correlation from causation in sector-emission relationships — required for legally defensible climate policy recommendations.
Feedback loop in practice: When a satellite U-Net detects a methane plume, the RL agent selects inspection priority. DGL-GNN forecasts downstream emission impact. SHAP explains the sector attribution. If a policy minister overrides the recommended intervention, the override feeds back as a reward signal — the system learns institutional constraints, exactly as coach overrides train sports RL agents.

Satellite + Computer Vision: Methane from Space

AI applications using satellite-mounted spectrometers detect and quantify greenhouse gas emissions at source — bypassing incomplete or politically manipulated ground-level reporting.

U-Net for Methane Plumes
Sentinel-5P TROPOMI data (3.5 × 5.5 km resolution) processed through encoder-decoder U-Net architectures to segment methane plumes. Detection threshold: ~1 tonne CH₄/hour.
Pangu-Weather (Microsoft)
3D Earth-specific Transformer trained on ERA5 reanalysis (1979–2018, 1B+ data points). Models atmospheric CO₂ transport, bypassing expensive PDE solvers. Inference: 1000× faster than NWP models.
Reinforcement Learning for Industry
MDP: State = (energy, production, emissions); Action = operational adjustments; Reward = carbon intensity minimisation. DeepMind demonstrated 40% cooling energy reduction in data centres.

Where Carbon AI Meets Sports Intelligence

These domains share an identical architectural substrate — and converge practically at events like the 2036 Olympics, which will itself be a massive carbon event:

Green Olympic Optimisation
RL agents optimising energy in 12 Olympic venues — minimising Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions while maintaining athlete comfort. Target: first net-zero Summer Olympics by 2036.
AI-Verified Carbon Credits
Satellite + U-Net pipelines verifying carbon offset projects funding Olympic sustainability. Machine learning ensures offsets are real, measurable, and permanent — combating greenwashing at national scale.
Athlete Thermal Adaptation
Transformer climate models (Pangu-Weather) feed directly into athlete heat acclimatisation protocols — merging climate forecasting with sports physiology AI as global temperatures rise.

The same Graph Neural Network that models carbon flows between industrial sectors can model energy flows between athletes, venues, and cities. Intelligence about the planet and intelligence about performance are converging on identical architectures — orchestrated by LLMs and executed by agents.

Takeaway

Implementation Roadmap for Climate Practitioners

For climate scientists and policymakers, the recommended architecture stack for 2026:

  • Stage 1: ARIMA-LSTM hybrids with LIME/SHAP baseline explainability
  • Stage 2: DGL-GNN + Temporal Transformer with attention-map XAI when relational data is available
  • Stage 3: Satellite U-Net + PPO/SAC RL for source detection and industrial emission control
  • Stage 4: Multi-agent orchestration with full RL + XAI feedback loops, counterfactual policy briefs, and human-in-the-loop reward shaping

The DGL framework reducing forecasting error by 15%+ is not marginal — at gigatonne scales, that precision translates directly into climate policy effectiveness worth trillions of dollars.
